Three threads of research

CZ·01 / D54

Individual differences in color perception

My dissertation. People differ in how they see color — that much is well documented. The open question is whether those differences are random, or whether there's structure to how we cluster. I use triadic similarity judgments to reconstruct each participant's perceptual color space, then test whether those perceptual differences predict behavior on interference and categorization-generalization tasks.

dissertation
CZ·02 / 2C4

How visualization design shapes interpretation

The visual features in a data visualization — hue, lightness, granularity, outlines — change what people infer, even with no key telling them what the visualization means. In one line of work, I ask participants to decide which side of an unlabeled colormap holds more data, with no definition of "more." Published work on colormap semantics; ongoing work with Sandia on hazard maps and evacuation decisions.

design × cognition
CZ·03 / 7A8

Applied research & program evaluation

At Sandia, I design surveys and conduct interviews to evaluate program effectiveness across three concurrent initiatives. The work taught me how research changes when the deliverable is an actionable recommendation, not a paper — how to write for a program director rather than a reviewer.
